WESR Triquest

(Wind Energy Safety Rules) is a vendor-neutral, open standard for safe planning, isolation, and operation in wind. It unifies best practice across HV/LV operations, Control of Hazardous Energy (LOTO), incident response, and training—so teams share one clear language and workflow, onshore and offshore. WESR is community-driven, transparent, and designed for real-world use in the nacelle, tower, and substation.

Browse rule sets Training hub

Mission

Make world-class safety practical: a clear, open ruleset that unifies HV/LV operations, CoHE/LOTO, incident response, and training—so organizations can plan, isolate, execute, and verify with confidence, offshore and onshore. Make safety rules and workflows accessible to everyone—through clear, audit-ready documents, open change logs, and a fair community process.

Open & Transparent

Open license with public change logs and decisions. Anyone can use, adapt, or fork—while the full history remains visible for audits, training, and cross-site consistency.

Neutral & Non-profit

Triquest stewards WESR as a vendor-neutral, non-profit effort with wide practitioner input—so the rules evolve on merit and field data, not on commercial priorities.

Standards-Aligned

Mapped to relevant EN/IEC/ISO and GWO requirements. Versioned cross-walks make it straightforward to evidence compliance and embed WESR into your existing governance.

Built for Work

Clear roles, actionable workflows, and ready-to-use forms/checklists. Designed to shorten briefings, reduce errors, and make isolation, switching, and sign-off faster and safer.

Governance & change process

Stewardship

Triquest performs non‑profit coordination and releases.

Community Council

Open council that reviews proposals, public comments and evidence.

Change Log

All documents carry version, date, and detailed Change of Record (CoR).

Proposal flow

  1. Open an issue/pull request describing problem, solution, and references.
  2. Public review (2–4 weeks): comments, evidence, pilot trials where possible.
  3. Approval: council decision; release with updated change log.

FAQ

Why is it called WESR Triquest?

To signal this is an open, non‑profit variant—separate from closed WESR initiatives.

Is this an official government document?

No. It is an open industry collaboration, aligned to standards but not a regulator.

Can we mix parts with our own procedures?

Yes. The documents are modular with clear interfaces to local workflows.

How do you manage responsibility and updates?

All changes go through an open process with versioning. Use is at your own risk per the licence.
