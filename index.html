WESR Triquest

(Wind Energy Safety Rules) is a vendor-neutral, open standard for safe planning, isolation, and operation in wind. It unifies best practice across HV/LV operations, Control of Hazardous Energy (LOTO), incident response, and training—so teams share one clear language and workflow, onshore and offshore. WESR is community-driven, transparent, and designed for real-world use in the nacelle, tower, and substation.

Browse rule sets Training hub

Mission

Make world-class safety practical: a clear, open ruleset that unifies HV/LV operations, CoHE/LOTO, incident response, and training—so organizations can plan, isolate, execute, and verify with confidence, offshore and onshore. Make safety rules and workflows accessible to everyone—through clear, audit-ready documents, open change logs, and a fair community process.

Open & Transparent

Open license with public change logs and decisions. Anyone can use, adapt, or fork—while the full history remains visible for audits, training, and cross-site consistency.

Neutral & Non-profit

Triquest stewards WESR as a vendor-neutral, non-profit effort with wide practitioner input—so the rules evolve on merit and field data, not on commercial priorities.

Standards-Aligned

Mapped to relevant EN/IEC/ISO and GWO requirements. Versioned cross-walks make it straightforward to evidence compliance and embed WESR into your existing governance.

Built for Work

Clear roles, actionable workflows, and ready-to-use forms/checklists. Designed to shorten briefings, reduce errors, and make isolation, switching, and sign-off faster and safer.

Governance & change process

Stewardship

WESR Triquest is coordinated by Triquest as a non-profit steward. The steward facilitates releases, maintains the repo, and supports the Community Council's decisions.

Community Council

An open council that reviews proposals, evidence and piloting results, and issues formal decisions recorded in the Change of Record.

Change Log

Each release carries a version, date, scope, and a detailed Change of Record (CoR). See CHANGELOG.

How to contribute

  1. Open an issue describing the gap/problem, proposed change, and references (standards, field evidence).
  2. Fork & open a pull request (PR) with concrete edits (Markdown/RTF/PDF) and a brief impact analysis.
  3. Respect the Code of Conduct and include a Sign-off line for the DCO in each commit.

Details: CONTRIBUTING.md · CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md

Decision-making

The Community Council applies an evidence-based process. After public review the council issues one of: Approve, Approve with changes, or Reject with rationale. The steward implements approved changes.

  • Public review: normally 2–4 weeks (can be shortened for urgent safety fixes).
  • Conflict of interest: council members must declare and recuse where relevant.
  • Transparency: decisions logged in GOVERNANCE.md and the CoR.

Releases & versioning

  • Versioning: MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH for documents and form packs (MAJOR = breaking workflow/role change; MINOR = new sections/forms; PATCH = corrections/clarity).
  • Change of Record (CoR): each release includes scope, rationale, affected docs/forms, and migration notes. See CHANGELOG.
  • Release cadence: monthly batched releases; urgent safety updates at any time.

Security & disclosures

Report safety-critical or confidentiality issues privately per SECURITY.md. We acknowledge within 5 business days and publish a public notice after remediation when appropriate.

Name & trademarks

"WESR Triquest" identifies this open, non-profit rule set. Usage must not imply endorsement or exclusivity. See TRADEMARKS.md.

Roles & responsibilities

  • Steward (Triquest): runs releases, maintains repo/site, facilitates reviews, ensures CoR quality.
  • Community Council: evaluates proposals, safeguards neutrality, approves/rejects changes.
  • Maintainers: subject-area reviewers for HV/LV/CoHE/Training; triage issues and PRs.
  • Contributors: propose changes and supply evidence (standards, incidents, trials).

See MAINTAINERS.md for current maintainers and areas.

FAQ

Why is it called WESR Triquest?

To signal this is an open, non‑profit variant—separate from closed WESR initiatives.

Is this an official government document?

No. It is an open industry collaboration, aligned to standards but not a regulator.

Can we mix parts with our own procedures?

Yes. The documents are modular with clear interfaces to local workflows.

How do you manage responsibility and updates?

All changes go through an open process with versioning. Use is at your own risk per the licence.
